What This System Does

The Medical Diagnostic Workbench takes a patient description — symptoms, vitals, lab values, and optionally an image — and processes it through a 5-stage AI pipeline. Each stage performs a different part of the diagnostic reasoning, from initial triage to a final clinical summary.

Behind the scenes, it uses Google's Agent Development Kit (ADK) to orchestrate multiple AI agents that work together — some in sequence, some in parallel, some in refinement loops. This is what makes it "agentic": the AI doesn't just answer a question, it decomposes the problem into stages and coordinates specialized agents to solve each part.

What Makes This "Agentic"?

Traditional AI gives you one answer to one question. An agentic system breaks problems into steps, assigns specialized AI agents to each, and coordinates them automatically. Here are the four patterns used:

Sequential
Steps in Order
Each stage finishes before the next starts. Triage must complete before diagnosis can begin.
Parallel
Work Simultaneously
Symptom analysis and image analysis run at the same time, saving time without losing quality.
Refinement Loop
Self-Improvement
The diagnosis agent generates possibilities, a critic evaluates them, and the process repeats until confident.
Smart Routing
Pick the Expert
The AI automatically decides which specialist is most relevant — no rules, just reasoning.
Case Walkthrough: Chest Pain

Here's what happens when a 58-year-old male with crushing chest pain enters the system:

Triage — Urgency Classification
The patient presents with crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis. The AI quickly classifies this as cardiac and assigns the highest urgency level. A hardcoded safety check also fires before any AI runs, detecting the emergency keyword combination.
EMERGENT • Category: Cardiac • Red flags: chest pain + SOB + diaphoresis
Parallel Analysis — Symptoms + Labs
Two agents work simultaneously. The symptom agent identifies the cardiac cluster (chest pain, dyspnea, elevated troponin 0.8 ng/mL, elevated CK-MB). It also computes eGFR from the creatinine value. The imaging agent notes no image was provided and recommends ECG + echocardiogram.
Troponin elevated • eGFR: 82.4 (normal) • No image provided
Diagnosis Refinement — Deep Reasoning
The generator produces a ranked differential: Acute MI at the top, followed by unstable angina, pulmonary embolism, and aortic dissection. The critic evaluates the evidence and confirms high confidence. The loop exits after 1–2 iterations because the clinical picture is clear.
#1 Acute MI (I21.9) 85% • #2 Unstable Angina (I20.0) 10% • Confidence: 0.88
Specialist Routing — Cardiology
The AI router examines the differential and routes to the Cardiology specialist. The cardiologist agent checks for drug interactions (lisinopril with potential contrast media) and contraindications, then provides a treatment recommendation.
Routed to: CardiologyAgent • Drug check: lisinopril + contrast (hold metformin 48h)
Case Summary — SOAP Note
All findings are aggregated into a structured clinical note: Subjective (patient complaints), Objective (vitals, labs, imaging), Assessment (diagnosis + specialist opinion), and Plan (treatment, medications, follow-up). A TIMI risk score is calculated.
SOAP note generated • TIMI score: High risk • Plan: emergent catheterization

Safety by Design

Every AI output carries a disclaimer. Life-threatening symptom combinations (chest pain + shortness of breath, stroke symptoms, anaphylaxis) trigger hardcoded emergency alerts that run before any AI call — these are deterministic rules, not AI-dependent. All patient data is fictional. No real medical data is processed.
