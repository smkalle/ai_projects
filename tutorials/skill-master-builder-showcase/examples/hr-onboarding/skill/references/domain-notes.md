## SHRM 4 C's Framework

The Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM) defines four pillars of effective onboarding:

### 1. Compliance
- All required legal and regulatory forms completed on time
- Federal: I-9 (Day 3 deadline), W-4
- State: new hire report, state tax withholding, pay notice
- Company: handbook acknowledgment, confidentiality agreement, benefits enrollment
- Documented in HRIS with timestamps

### 2. Clarification
- New hire understands their exact role, responsibilities, and KPIs
- Written 30-60-90 day expectations document delivered by Day 5
- Performance standards defined before end of Week 1
- Weekly 1:1 with manager throughout first 90 days

### 3. Culture
- Company values, mission, and behavioral norms communicated actively
- Culture conveyed by team behavior — not just HR orientation slides
- New hire participates in team rituals within Week 1 (standups, all-hands, etc.)
- Psychological safety established early: errors are learning, questions are welcome

### 4. Connection
- Buddy/mentor assigned before Day 1 — not on Day 1
- At least 3 cross-functional introductions in Week 1
- Manager available for daily 15-min check-ins for first 30 days
- Sense of belonging correlates directly with 90-day retention

## Onboarding Phases Overview

| Phase | Timeline | Owner | Primary Goal |
|-------|----------|-------|--------------|
| Pre-Boarding | T-14 to T-1 | HR + Manager + IT | Logistics, compliance prep, welcome |
| Day 1 | Day 1 | Manager + HR | First impression, I-9, system access |
| Week 1 | Days 2–7 | Manager + Buddy | Role clarity, training launch, culture integration |
| 30-Day Checkpoint | Day 28–32 | Manager + HR | Early performance signal, feedback collection |
| 60-Day Review | Day 58–62 | Manager | Independence assessment, career development |
| 90-Day Close | Day 88–92 | Manager + HR | Formal evaluation, onboarding record closed |

**Full onboarding duration:** 90 days minimum; complete cultural integration may take 6–12 months.

## Key Success Metrics

### Primary KPIs (track for every hire)

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Time-to-Productivity (TTP) | Days until new hire works independently | Role-dependent: 30–90 days typical |
| 90-Day Retention | % of new hires still employed at Day 90 | 90%+ (industry avg: 67%) |
| Training Completion Rate | % completing all mandatory modules by Day 30 | 100% |
| Onboarding Satisfaction Score | Survey score from 30-day check-in | 8/10 or above |
| Manager Engagement | Weekly 1:1 completion rate | 100% for first 90 days |

### Secondary KPIs

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Time-to-First Contribution | First meaningful deliverable within 30 days |
| 6-Month Retention | 85%+ |
| Promotion Readiness at 12 months | Track internal mobility |
| Cost of Failed Onboarding | 50–200% of annual salary per replacement |

### Measurement Cadence
- Week 1: Pulse survey (3 questions max — don't overload)
- Day 30: Comprehensive onboarding survey
- Day 90: Full new hire experience survey
- Compare results across cohorts by role, manager, department

## Common Failure Points

### Ranked by frequency and business impact:

1. **IT not ready on Day 1** — No email, no access = immediate disengagement
   - Prevention: IT checklist initiated at T-14, verified at T-2

2. **Manager disengaged** — New hires with engaged managers are 3.5x more likely to report exceptional onboarding
   - Prevention: Lock manager commitment before hire starts; include onboarding in manager KPIs

3. **Unclear expectations** — Top cause of early resignation within 90 days
   - Prevention: Written 30-60-90 day plan delivered by Day 5, not Day 30

4. **I-9 compliance missed** — Federal fines $272–$2,701 per first offense, per form
   - Prevention: Calendar reminder at hire for Day 3 deadline; HR signs off explicitly

5. **Information overload in Week 1** — Cognitive overload reduces training retention
   - Prevention: Spread training across 30 days; prioritize mandatory compliance first

6. **No feedback loop** — Problems invisible until resignation
   - Prevention: Week 1 pulse survey + 30-day comprehensive survey mandatory

7. **Buddy not briefed** — Buddy assigned but doesn't know their role
   - Prevention: Buddy briefing session before new hire starts; use buddy-assignment-form.md

8. **Job description mismatch** — Actual role differs from what was advertised
   - Prevention: Manager presents actual day-to-day responsibilities in Day 1 1:1

## Buddy/Mentor Program Standards

### Selection Criteria
- NOT the direct manager (buddy should be peer, not authority)
- Tenure of 6+ months (knows organizational nuances)
- High performer and culture carrier
- Availability for 30-min/day commitment in Week 1, tapering to weekly by Day 30

### Buddy Responsibilities
- Informal check-ins daily (Week 1), 2x/week (Weeks 2–4), weekly (Days 30–90)
- Answer "dumb questions" the new hire fears asking the manager
- Introduce new hire to informal networks and cultural norms
- Provide honest feedback on culture fit and team dynamics to HR (not manager)
- Alert HR if new hire shows disengagement signals

### What Buddy is NOT Responsible For
- Performance management
- Compliance or paperwork
- Formal training delivery
- Replacing manager 1:1s

## Remote vs. On-Site Differences

| Onboarding Element | On-Site | Remote |
|-------------------|---------|--------|
| Equipment | Workstation ready Day 1 | Ship equipment with tracking; confirm delivery T-3 |
| Day 1 Intro | In-person tour + team lunch | Virtual platform walkthrough + video coffee chat |
| I-9 Section 2 | In-person document inspection | Authorized representative or remote I-9 service required |
| Buddy Check-ins | Impromptu desk visits | Scheduled video calls (don't go async) |
| Culture Integration | Organic informal interactions | Must be intentional: virtual coffees, team Slack channels |
| Week 1 Overload Risk | Lower (informal anchors help) | Higher (video fatigue — cap at 4 hrs of video/day) |

### Remote-Specific Additions
- Set up: webcam, headset, home office stipend (if applicable)
- Provide written guide: company Slack channels, meeting norms, async communication expectations
- Ensure VPN setup is tested before Day 1
- Schedule "virtual open door" time with manager (not just formal 1:1s)

## Role-Specific Customization

### Engineering / Technical
- TTP: 60–90 days typical
- Week 1 priority: dev environment setup, repo access, architecture overview
- 30-day goal: complete one ticket or contribute to one sprint
- 90-day goal: independently scoped and delivered feature or bug fix

### Sales
- TTP: 90 days (ramp to quota)
- Week 1 priority: CRM access, product training, ICP (ideal customer profile) review
- 30-day goal: shadow 5+ calls, deliver solo demo
- 90-day goal: 50–75% of quota attainment

### Customer Support / Operations
- TTP: 30–45 days
- Week 1 priority: ticketing system, escalation paths, top 20 issue types
- 30-day goal: handle tier-1 tickets independently
- 90-day goal: full queue ownership, CSAT aligned with team average

### People / HR
- TTP: 30–60 days
- Week 1 priority: HRIS access, payroll cycle, benefits systems
- 30-day goal: run or support one HR process end-to-end
- 90-day goal: own a recurring HR workflow independently
