## Federal Requirements

### Form I-9 — Employment Eligibility Verification
- **Who:** All employees hired after November 6, 1986
- **Employee Section 1:** Must be completed on or before first day of work for pay
- **Employer Section 2:** Must be completed within 3 business days of start date
- **Fines:** $272–$2,701 per paperwork violation (first offense); up to $27,018 for knowing violations
- **Retention:** 3 years from hire date OR 1 year after separation — whichever is LATER
- **Storage:** Must be available for ICE/DHS audit within 72 hours of request

### Form W-4 — Federal Tax Withholding
- **When:** Completed before or on first day
- **2020+ version:** Uses new worksheet format (no longer personal allowances)
- **If not submitted:** Withhold as Single with no adjustments (federal default)
- **Updates:** Employee can update W-4 at any time; employer must implement within pay period

### Form W-4 for Non-Resident Aliens
- Must also complete Form 8233 or claim treaty benefits
- Different withholding rules apply — flag for payroll team

### EEOC Anti-Discrimination Compliance
- Onboarding process must not discriminate on basis of: race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, sexual orientation), national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic information
- Documentation must be consistent across all hires
- Do not ask about: age, birthdate (until after hire for benefits), citizenship (until I-9), disability status (until after conditional offer), family status

### Direct Deposit Authorization
- Federal law does not require direct deposit; however, most states allow mandating it
- Employee must be given a written alternative payment method option in states that require it

### Emergency Contact Form
- Not federally required but best practice for all hires
- Must not be used for discriminatory purposes (don't use to determine marital status, family status)

### Required Workplace Postings
All employers must display (physical location or electronic for remote):
- EEOC "Know Your Rights" poster
- FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act) poster
- OSHA "Job Safety and Health" poster
- FMLA poster (if 50+ employees)
- NLRA "Employee Rights" poster
- Uniformed Services Employment poster (USERRA)
- State-specific postings (vary by state — see state section below)

## State New Hire Reporting

**ALL 50 states require new hire reporting.** This is a federal mandate (Personal Responsibility and Work Opportunity Reconciliation Act of 1996) administered through states.

### Requirements
- **Who must report:** All employers (no size threshold)
- **What to report:** New hire name, address, SSN, start date; employer name, address, EIN
- **Purpose:** Child support enforcement and benefits fraud detection

### Deadlines by State (selected — verify current rules)

| State | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| California | 20 days from hire date |
| New York | 20 days from hire date |
| Texas | 20 days from hire date |
| Florida | 20 days from hire date |
| Illinois | 20 days from hire date |
| Most states | 20 days from hire date |
| Some states | As soon as 2 pay periods after hire |

**Default safe rule:** File within 20 days of start date in all states.

### How to File
- Most states: Online portal or through HRIS system integration
- HRIS systems (Workday, BambooHR, Rippling) typically auto-file if configured

## Document Retention Rules

| Document | Retention Period |
|----------|-----------------|
| Form I-9 | 3 years from hire OR 1 year post-separation (whichever is longer) |
| W-4 | 4 years after the tax year it applies to |
| Payroll records | 3 years (FLSA) |
| Benefits enrollment records | 6 years (ERISA) |
| Employment applications | 1–3 years (varies by state and EEOC) |
| Background check results | 5 years (FCRA) |
| Performance reviews / 30-60-90 evaluations | Duration of employment + 3 years |
| Training completion records | Duration of employment + 3 years |
| Signed handbook acknowledgment | Duration of employment + 3 years |

## State-Specific Notable Requirements

### California
- **Wage and Hour Notice (Labor Code 2810.5):** Written notice at hire of: pay rate, overtime status, pay frequency, employer name and address
- **CFRA/FMLA notice:** Must provide if 5+ employees (California threshold is lower than federal)
- **Sexual harassment prevention training:** Required within 6 months of hire (supervisors: 2 hours; non-supervisors: 1 hour)
- **Paid Sick Leave notice:** Must inform employees of accrual policy at hire

### New York
- **Wage Theft Prevention Act:** Written notice of pay rate, overtime eligibility, pay frequency — must be in employee's primary language AND English
- **Paid Family Leave notice:** Provide policy at hire
- **Sexual harassment training:** Annual training required; can be integrated into onboarding

### Texas
- No state income tax — skip state tax withholding form
- New hire report to Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) within 20 days
- Workers' compensation is not mandatory in Texas (employer must post notice of coverage decision)

### Florida
- No state income tax — skip state tax withholding form
- New hire report to Florida Department of Revenue within 20 days
- E-Verify required for public employers and some state contractors

### Illinois
- **Illinois Human Rights Act:** Broader anti-discrimination protections than federal EEOC
- **One Day Rest in Seven Act:** Applicable to scheduling — note for shift workers
- State income tax form: IL-W-4

### Washington State
- **WA Cares Fund:** Long-term care insurance payroll deduction — inform new hires
- State income tax: none, but has paid family and medical leave — provide notice
- L&I registration required for workers' compensation

## I-9 Compliance Deep Dive

### Acceptable Documents (List A — establishes both identity AND work authorization)
- US Passport or Passport Card
- Permanent Resident Card (Green Card, Form I-551)
- Employment Authorization Document (Form I-766)
- Foreign passport with I-551 stamp or I-94 notation
- Passport from Federated States of Micronesia or Marshall Islands

### Acceptable Documents (List B — identity only, must use with List C)
List B examples:
- Driver's license or state ID card
- Federal, state, or local government ID
- School ID with photograph
- Voter registration card

List C examples:
- Social Security card (unrestricted)
- Birth certificate (US)
- US Citizen ID Card (Form I-197)

### Common I-9 Mistakes That Generate Fines
1. Section 2 completed late (after Day 3)
2. Employer writes employee's information in Section 1
3. Accepting expired documents (not permitted — I-9 requires unexpired docs)
4. Requesting more documents than required
5. Reverifying documents that don't require reverification (US Passports, Green Cards for existing LPRs)
6. Not recording document numbers and expiration dates accurately
7. Missing employer signature in Section 2
8. White-out used to correct errors (not allowed — draw line through error, write correction, initial and date)

### I-9 Audit Preparation
- Keep I-9s separate from personnel files (makes audits easier)
- Use an I-9 audit spreadsheet to track expiration dates for reverification
- Run internal I-9 audits annually — correct errors proactively

## Remote I-9 Completion

Since COVID-19, DHS has authorized remote I-9 completion for E-Verify employers. Options:

### Option 1: Authorized Representative
- Employer designates any person (notary, CPA, HR consultant, trusted local contact) as authorized representative
- Authorized rep physically inspects documents in person with employee
- Authorized rep completes Section 2 on employer's behalf
- Employer remains liable for any errors

### Option 2: Remote Document Examination (E-Verify employers only)
- As of August 2023, DHS allows remote examination for E-Verify participants
- Employee transmits document images via video, fax, or electronic means
- Employer reviews documents remotely and completes Section 2
- Must retain copies of all documents examined remotely
- Mark "remote examination" in Section 2 additional information field

### Option 3: Third-Party I-9 Service
- Companies like Tracker I-9, Equifax I-9 Anywhere, or HireRight provide managed remote I-9 completion
- Employee visits a staffed location or conducts video appointment
- Fully compliant and auditable

**Recommendation:** For remote hires, establish one of these three options before Day 1. Do not skip or delay I-9 for remote employees.
